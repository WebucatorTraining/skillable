# Exercise 1 – Create a new SharePoint Site
<p class="block_">We will now set up your first SharePoint team site, used throughout this course.</p>
<ol class="list_">
<li class="block_20">In the <b class="calibre8">app launcher</b> <b class="calibre8"><img alt="Image" class="calibre43" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-2.jpeg"/></b> click on <b class="calibre8">SharePoint</b> (if a tutorial appears, check out the tips)<br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Next, click on the <b class="calibre8">+ Create site</b> button at the top of the site (this may be hidden if you are on a low-resolution monitor, you can try changing the zoom level on your browser to show this button)<br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="A screenshot of a computer  Description automatically generated with low confidence" class="calibre739" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-13.jpeg"/><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">A large pop-out window will display 2 options for you to select from, either <i class="calibre11">Team Site</i> or <i class="calibre11">Communication Site</i>, choose <b class="calibre8">Team site</b>. Enter a <i class="calibre11">Site-name</i> (we recommend entering your first name and then your team, department, business unit or division) for example <b class="calibre8">Chris-Training</b> or <b class="calibre8">Caroline-Sales</b><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Enter a suitable description to inform visitors what content they are likely to find when visiting this site, try to use keywords that describe the team as this can help with searching <br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">For the <i class="calibre11">Privacy settings</i> choose <b class="calibre8">Private</b> (we can choose to add visitors later once the site is set up)<br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Click <b class="calibre8">Next</b>, you will now see the <i class="calibre11">Who do you want to add? </i>screen, here, under <i class="calibre11">Add additional owners</i>, add <b class="calibre8">Steve Smith<br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/></b><img alt="A screenshot of a computer  Description automatically generated" class="calibre740" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-801.png"/></li>
<li class="block_42">Click <b class="calibre8">Finish</b> to access your new site</li>
</ol>

# Exercise 2 – Update the navigation
<p class="block_">To make it easier to navigate between the various web sites we will add some new entries to your site navigation.</p>
<ol class="list_">
<li class="block_20">After creating a new site, you are presented with the <i class="calibre11">Next steps </i>pane, click <b class="calibre8">X</b> to close it<br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">In the left navigation of your new site, take a look at the quick launch (left menu) at the bottom of the quick launch, click on the <b class="calibre8">Edit</b> button<br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="A screen shot of a computer  Description automatically generated with medium confidence" class="calibre741" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-802.png"/><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">The quick launch is now in edit mode, hover your mouse cursor beneath a link, for example, <i class="calibre11">Home</i> to reveal the <b class="calibre8">Add a link</b> button and click on it<br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="Image" class="calibre742" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image1068.png"/><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Note that the drop-down at the top of the menu allows you to instantly link to tools in the <i class="calibre11">Microsoft 365 Group</i> associated with this site, here leave <b class="calibre8">Link</b> selected<br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">In the address bar, we will create a link to an external website; you can choose your company’s website if you like or if you want an example try: <br class="calibre7"/><b class="calibre8">http://www.combined-knowledge.com</b> or, enter the web address for your organisation<br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">In the Display name field enter a description, for example, your companies name or to continue the example from earlier <b class="calibre8">On Demand Training</b>, or enter <b class="calibre8">My Company</b> if you added a link to your organisation in step 5<br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Click <b class="calibre8">OK</b> to create the link, click on the ellipsis (…) to the right of the new link for management options<br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Use <b class="calibre8">Move up </b>or <b class="calibre8">Move down </b>to position the link, if it is not under <i class="calibre11">Home</i> already, position it under the <i class="calibre11">Home</i> link<br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Next choose <b class="calibre8">Make sub link</b> to indent the link under the home link<br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_42">At the bottom of the menu, click <b class="calibre8">Save</b> and notice that sub-links collapse, consider using sub-links to better structure the quick launch (if you don’t see the drop-down arrow next to the <i class="calibre11">Home</i> link try refreshing your browser)</li>
</ol>

The staff that travel and accumulate personal expenses for their work can claim it back through this library.</p>

# Lab 6 – Customising Security
<p class="block_"><img alt="Image" class="calibre227" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-642.png"/></p>
<p class="block_"><span class="calibre17">As your site grows and more content is created, the importance of ensuring the information reaches the right audience becomes the next stage in our sites story. Typically, very few people will have access to the site. As time goes on, changes will happen to the site and its members  that may require a shift in the way users access information.</span></p>
<p class="block_">A new site owner should check their sites publicity and its sharing settings. This will allow you to control if people can automatically join your site, and how much of the site they are allowed to share. More restrictive sites can allow access requests, we will configure this option to allow those who do not have access to our site the option to request access from our site owners.</p>
<p class="block_">Next we will look at sharing the site. You can either just share your site, or if you are using a Team site with a Microsoft 365 group, you can choose to share your site and associated shared mailbox, group calendar and Team if present. In this exercise we will define reasons for whether we will need to add someone at the site or group level. </p>
<p class="block_">Lastly we will look at rights around apps, folders and files. In SharePoint you can be both permissive and restrictive in access for containers within a site. Much of these customisations can be managed within the modern SharePoint interface, however, for certain actions we will require the use of the classic interface.</p>

# Exercise 2 – Managing Microsoft 365 Groups
<p class="block_">SharePoint Team sites favour being managed by Microsoft 365 Groups. This is due to the team site template being the backbone of the Microsoft 365 Groups object, for example when a Team is created in Microsoft Teams, a Microsoft 365 Group is created. The SharePoint site, within the group, is used in the background to store the Teams files. Hence SharePoint teams sites is integral to Microsoft 365 groups. Some site templates do not use Microsoft 365 groups like the Communication site template. # Exercise 3 – Managing SharePoint Groups
<p class="block_">When working in Microsoft 365 Groups, you might find that some audiences may not need access to all of the resources in a group. Elements of a Microsoft 365 Group like the mailbox, calendar, team (if present) may not be something you wish to share with some colleagues due to the nature of the information being discussed. To share only the site, and not the other services tied to a Microsoft 365 Group, you can use the same interface we just used to share the site.</p>
<ol class="list_">
<li class="block_20">The <i class="calibre11">Settings</i> pane should still be open from the previous exercise, otherwise click on the <b class="calibre8">Settings</b> <img alt="Single gear outline" class="calibre825" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-877.png"/> cog at the top-right of the page <br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Next choose <b class="calibre8">Site permissions</b><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Click on the <b class="calibre8">Add members</b> button<br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Select the <b class="calibre8">Share site only</b> button<br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="A screenshot of a computer  Description automatically generated" class="calibre830" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-885.png"/><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">We removed Caroline Lee earlier who previously had access to the Microsoft 365 Group, lets add her once more to this site, type <b class="calibre8">Caroline Lee</b> into the search box and select the result<br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Also add <b class="calibre8">Simon Gray</b> another user we wish to share the site with<br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Make both Caroline and Simon visitors by selecting their level of access <b class="calibre8">Edit</b> and adjusting it to <b class="calibre8">Read<br class="calibre7"/> </b></li>
<li class="block_39">Untick the option <b class="calibre8">Send email</b><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Lastly click <b class="calibre8">Add</b><br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="A screenshot of a computer  Description automatically generated" class="calibre833" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-886.png"/><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Now that both Caroline and Simon have access to just the site, and not the other services in the Microsoft 365 Group, Caroline reports that she needs to be able to work on documents within the site, expand the group called <b class="calibre8">Site visitors – no control<br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/></b><img alt="A black and white text  Description automatically generated" class="calibre834" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-887.png"/><b class="calibre8"><br class="calibre7"/> </b></li>
<li class="block_39">Click on the <b class="calibre8">Read</b> drop down below <i class="calibre11">Caroline Lee</i> then choose <b class="calibre8">Edit</b><br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="Image" class="calibre835" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-888.png"/><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Although Caroline disappears from the site visitors group, you can expand the <b class="calibre8">Site members – limited control</b> to note that Caroline is now in this group<br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="A group of circles with text and a yellow arrow  Description automatically generated" class="calibre836" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-889.png"/><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Note that there is an additional group in Site members called <i class="calibre11">[Site name] Members</i>, these are the users that you have made Members of your Microsoft 365 Group, their access can collectively be increased to Owner or decreased to Read but it is very uncommon to do this as it would negatively impact collaboration<br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="A screenshot of a website  Description automatically generated" class="calibre837" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-890.png"/></li>
<li class="block_42">Click on the <b class="calibre8">X</b> at the top right of the <i class="calibre11">Permissions</i> pane to close it and do the same for the <i class="calibre11">Settings</i> pane</li>
</ol>

# Exercise 4 – Manage access to an app
<p class="block_">Our expenses library should not be visible to the visitors of our site like Simon. We have been asked to hide the whole library so that visitors are unable to see the library in the navigation or search results. This can be achieved by stopping inheritance of the sites security within the app, allowing us to remove the Site visitors group.</p>
<ol class="list_">
<li class="block_20">In your left navigation, click on <b class="calibre8">Site contents</b> and in the site contents menu, click <b class="calibre8">Expenses</b> (or if you added the link to the Expenses library into the quick launch, use it to access the Expenses library)<br class="calibre7"/> </li>
</ol>
<ol class="list_">
<li class="block_39">Click on the <b class="calibre8">Settings</b> <img alt="Single gear outline" class="calibre825" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-877.png"/> cog at the top-right of the page, and select <b class="calibre8">Library settings</b><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">In the <i class="calibre11">Library settings</i> pane choose <b class="calibre8">More library settings</b><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Under the heading <i class="calibre11">Permissions and Management</i> click on the link for <b class="calibre8">Permissions for this document library</b><br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="A screenshot of a computer  Description automatically generated" class="calibre838" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-891.png"/></li>
<li class="block_39">In the ribbon, click on the option, <b class="calibre8">Stop inheriting permissions</b><br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="A screenshot of a computer screen  Description automatically generated" class="calibre839" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-892.png"/><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_21">When asked to confirm, click <b class="calibre8">OK</b><br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="A grey background with white text  Description automatically generated" class="calibre840" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-893.png"/></li>
</ol>
<p class="block_9" id="calibre_pb_295"> </p>

<ol class="list_">
<li class="block_20" value="6">Although the library is no longer inheriting its security, the groups can still be populated with users at this level, which directly impacts the whole site, if anyone needs to be added as a user to just this library, it is best practice to use the Grant permissions button in the ribbon rather than opening any of the listed groups and adding the user there<br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">To remove the visitors group from this library, place a tick next to the <b class="calibre8">[Site name] Visitors</b> group<br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="A screenshot of a computer  Description automatically generated" class="calibre841" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-894.png"/><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">In the ribbon select <b class="calibre8">Remove User Permissions</b><br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="A screenshot of a computer  Description automatically generated" class="calibre842" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-895.png"/><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Click <b class="calibre8">OK </b>to confirm<br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Now only the Members and Owners of this site can see the expenses library, note that to undo any changes in inheritance, the <i class="calibre11">Delete unique permissions</i> button can be used to reset the security for this app<br class="calibre7"/> </li>
</ol>

# Exercise 5 – Restricting access to a folder
<p class="block_">A member of your site, Caroline, has asked if a special folder can be set up for management. This folder should only be accessible to the team leaders Steve, Caroline and yourself. In this scenario you will create a folder and use modern group management to adjust the audience of the folder.</p>
<ol class="list_">
<li class="block_20">In the quick launch select the <b class="calibre8">Documents</b> library<br class="calibre7"/> </li>
</ol>
<ol class="list_">
<li class="block_39" value="2">Select <b class="calibre8">New</b> and choose <b class="calibre8">Folder</b><br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="Image" class="calibre843" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-896.png"/><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Name the folder <b class="calibre8">Private – Management Only</b> and click <b class="calibre8">Create</b><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_21">Right-click on the folder and choose <b class="calibre8">Manage access</b><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="A computer screen with a mouse pointer  Description automatically generated" class="calibre844" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-897.png"/><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
</ol>
<p class="block_9" id="calibre_pb_297"> </p>

<ol class="list_">
<li class="block_20" value="5">In the <i class="calibre11">Manage access</i> menu, select the <b class="calibre8">Groups</b> tab<br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="A screenshot of a computer screen  Description automatically generated" class="calibre845" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-898.png"/><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">At this point, note that the visitors and members groups both have access to the folder, to reduce this down click on the <b class="calibre8">Can view</b> option to the right of <i class="calibre11">[Site Name] – Visitors</i><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="A black and white image of a number  Description automatically generated" class="calibre846" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-899.png"/></li>
<li class="block_39">Expand the <b class="calibre8">Direct access</b> drop down and select <b class="calibre8">Can view</b>, then choose <b class="calibre8">Remove direct access</b> (this is the same as stop inheriting permissions in classic SharePoint AND removing the group at the same time)<br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="A screenshot of a computer  Description automatically generated" class="calibre847" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-900.png"/><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Confirm by clicking <b class="calibre8">Remove</b><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Repeat steps 6 to 8 to remove the <i class="calibre11">[Site Name] – Members </i>group<br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">You will just be left with the owners groups which cannot be removed, this ensures that owners can always support all elements of their site, even ones with custom permissions<br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="A screenshot of a phone  Description automatically generated" class="calibre848" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-901.png"/><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">To make sure that Caroline and Steve both have access to the folder, we will add them using the <b class="calibre8">Grant access</b> button at the top right of the menu<br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="A close-up of a person's face  Description automatically generated" class="calibre849" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-902.png"/><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Enter Caroline Lee and Steve smith into the To field<br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="A screenshot of a chat  Description automatically generated" class="calibre850" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-903.png"/><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Choose <b class="calibre8">Grant access</b>, this will email anyone who doesn’t currently have access, like Caroline<br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Close the confirmation dialog by clicking <b class="calibre8">X</b><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Right click on the folder again and choose <b class="calibre8">Manage access</b><br class="calibre7"/> </li>
<li class="block_39">Notice that Steve Smith and Caroline Lee are both editors<br class="calibre7"/><br class="calibre7"/><img alt="A screenshot of a group  Description automatically generated" class="calibre851" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-904.png"/><br class="calibre7"/> </li>

# Lab 7 – Working with Search
<p class="block_"><img alt="Image" class="calibre33" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-687.png"/></p>
<p class="block_">To help locate content that resides in apps across Microsoft 365 such as Microsoft Teams, SharePoint and OneDrive, you can use search. Search can be conducted very locally, inside an app such as a list or library. You can also conduct searches for information at the site level and even from all sites, depending on your level of access. As discussed, when searching for content, you will only see information that you have been given access to. If you do not have access to a site, its contents will not appear in your search. This is known as security trimming.</p>
<p class="block_">Your searches will also allow you to filter your content further so that many results can reduce to a handful in one or two clicks.</p>
<p class="block_">Modern search is always being improved, and we can expect more rich features as well as the ability to customise the way it works in the future.</p>
<p class="block_">Classic search can be accessed through legacy SharePoint links which will allow privileged users to make customisations. The enterprise search centre has been created as part of your Microsoft 365 tenant. Although at present this has been completely overridden by modern search.</p>
<p class="block_">When further customisations capabilities are added to the modern search interface, we will endeavour to improve this exercise with these techniques.</p>

# Lab 8 – Enterprise Content Management
<p class="block_" id="id_Toc459914173"><img alt="Image" class="calibre6" src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/WebucatorTraining/skillable/main/55215/epub/images/image-785.png"/></p>
<p class="block_">The success of your SharePoint site is reaching other areas of the business. They have their own SharePoint sites and would like their own expense claim process. Rather than rebuild the library for each team and encourage them to keep using the most up to date expense claim template, you decide to band together with stakeholders to discuss a global expense claim.</p>
<p class="block_">The team have agreed on an up to date template as well as required properties that should be appended to each claim. This is to help keep track of the business processes around completing a claim.</p>
