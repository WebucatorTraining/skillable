Lab 2 – Getting started

In this lab we will look at building our first list. We will use a template to begin with and then expand the capabilities of the list even further. We then enable version control allowing us to access item history and roll back changes. We will also introduce some new columns to our list to complement the ones that came with the template. We will then update our view to hide some of the columns we do not need to see in the list. Next, we will add some new items to our list and use the version control we enabled earlier to recover from a mistake. We will also look at how to delete and recover list items. Lastly, we will get you to share your list with another user in your Microsoft 365 tenant.

Exercise 2 – Enable versioning

Now that we have our list, it is a good practice to enable versioning. This allows the list to record changes made to items. This is essentially an audit trail of changes that we can review and even recover from.

  1. In your new list, click on the Settings cog at the top right of the page
     
  2. Choose List settings


     
  3. Select Versioning settings which is located under the General Settings heading
     
  4. For the option Item Version History change the option for Create a version each time you edit an item in this list? To Yes
     
  5. In the box titled Keep the following number of versions enter 100 (you can save up to 50,000 most recent versions)

  6. At the bottom of the page, click OK
     
  7. At the top of the page, return to the list by clicking Job applications in the breadcrumb trail

  8. You list now has versioning enabled

Lab 3 – Add advanced columns and apply formatting

In our marketing site we have been interested in turning some of their spreadsheets into lists. We want to leverage the mobile access of the lists and the workflows in Microsoft Lists. One of delays in moving to Microsoft Lists is that Excel has conditional formatting and the calculations. We can advise the marketing team that we will attempt to recreate this aesthetic and functionality in a list.

The Excel spreadsheet in question is for an upcoming recruitment campaign for IT services roles. Previously in our company we outsourced our IT services, however we are bringing this service in house. To ensure we are hiring the new staff at a competitive salary we have researched similar job roles in various cities in England.

  8. At the bottom of the form click OK

Lab 5 – Setting up rules and designing flows

In our last lab we will be exploring the options for automated business processes within our lists. Like customising forms, there are different tools for adding automated business processes to a list depending on how customised the process needs to be. Simpler processes such as being notified when an item is deleted can be easily added using a rule. However, for a more bespoke process, such as being notified in Teams if one of your staff is booking time off with a choice to approve or reject and notify them by teams will require Power Automate. This is a workflow design tool, and we can build workflows, known simply as flows in Power Automate quite quickly by leveraging templates.
